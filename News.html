8 Tips For Better Fall Portrait Photos

    Scout your location close to shoot day You can look out your window and see amazing foliage today, but depending on the weather, it could literally almost all be gone by tomorrow. All it takes is one frost or some strong wind and your amazing autumn backdrop becomes a skeletal arrangement of bare branches that looks like something right out of True Detective. Scouting your spot is basic portrait photography 101, but don't wait a whole week between your scouting trip and the shoot. Colors can change, leaves can fall, some places like parks even close off sections for the winter. The Weather Channel actually keeps a pretty handy regional guide that lets you know what phase the leaves are in at any given time.
